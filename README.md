Pig Farming Guide

 1. Planning and Setup
- Choose a good location:
  - Well-drained and accessible.
  - Away from residential areas.
- Build suitable housing:
  - Ventilated pens.
  - Feeding and watering systems.
- Decide on the scale:
  - Small, medium, or commercial farm.

 2. Breeding and Stocking
- Select healthy pig breeds suited for your climate.
- Buy piglets or breeding stock from trusted farms.
- Ensure animals are vaccinated before stocking.

 3. Feeding and Nutrition
- Provide balanced feed:
  - Carbohydrates, proteins, vitamins, and minerals.
- Supply clean water at all times.
- Adjust rations for:
  - Piglets.
  - Growers.
  - Sows.

 4. Health Management
- Regular vaccinations and deworming.
- Maintain hygiene in pens to prevent diseases.
- Ensure access to a veterinary doctor.

 5. Daily Management
- Clean pens regularly.
- Monitor pig growth and behavior.
- Record feed intake, weight, and health status.

 6. Breeding and Farrowing
- Keep breeding records.
- Provide special care for pregnant sows.
- Ensure piglets are kept warm and fed colostrum.

 7. Marketing and Sales
- Decide whether to sell:
  - Piglets.
  - Pork.
  - Processed products.
- Build connections with:
  - Local markets.
  - Restaurants.
  - Processors.
- Monitor costs and profits.
